# Material modeler — Substance-S in Medium-M

Each snippet below is an independent observation record. Cite by the
bracketed ID when producing findings.

## [material_01]

Substance-S is introduced as a localized pulse into Medium-M and its
concentration profile is measured spatially as time advances. Initially
concentrated near a point, the profile widens symmetrically outward as time
progresses. When Medium-M is at rest, no shift of the profile's center is
observed; only spreading. The spatial spread of the profile grows with
elapsed time, and longer elapsed times produce wider profiles than shorter
elapsed times.

## [material_02]

When Medium-M is given a uniform bulk velocity, the center of the
concentration profile translates at that velocity while the profile
continues to spread at the same rate observed in the stationary case. The
bulk translation and the spreading are additive and independently
characterized: the translation proceeds at a fixed rate per unit time while
the profile spreading continues to grow over time. Changing the bulk
velocity affects the center but not the spreading rate.

## [material_03]

Measured at the microscopic scale, tiny concentration transfers between
adjacent thin zones of Medium-M appear statistically independent across
consecutive short time windows: what crosses one zonal boundary in a short
window carries no information about what will cross the next boundary in
the subsequent window. The aggregate profile, assembled from these
independent micro-transfers, converges to a smooth symmetric single-peaked
shape regardless of the precise shape of the initial pulse, provided the
pulse is localized and enough time has elapsed for many micro-transfers to
occur.

## [material_04]

Across repeated experiments with the same initial condition and the same
elapsed duration t, the profile observed at time t is statistically
reproducible: its spread and shape are predicted from elapsed duration
alone. Longer elapsed times produce reproducibly wider profiles. The
evolution preserves its qualitative structure under a joint rescaling of
the spatial axis and the time axis by appropriately matched factors.
